About Swimming

Swimming's history extends back to prehistoric times, with evidence suggesting it was practiced as early as 7,000 years ago. Early records include Stone Age paintings and depictions in ancient texts. Competitive swimming emerged in Europe in the 1800s and became part of the first modern Olympic Games in 1896. The sport continues to evolve, with organizations like FINA (now World Aquatics) playing a role in governing and promoting swimming internationally.


Newton's Third Law of Motion states that for every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction. Thus, swimmers must stroke downward in the water to stay afloat and propel forward. This movement is equal and opposite to the force the water exerts against the swimmer to stop them from moving.
